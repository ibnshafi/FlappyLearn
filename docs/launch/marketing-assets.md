## Launch Announcement

FlappyLearn is now public: a neuroevolution playground where an AI learns Flappy Bird from scratch.

It includes a deterministic simulator, evolving recurrent neural circuits, training metrics, replay exports, benchmarks, tests, and beginner-friendly docs explaining the machine-learning ideas behind the system.

Run a smoke experiment in minutes, open the replay in your browser, and watch evolution discover flap timing one generation at a time.

## Reddit Launch Post

Title: I open-sourced FlappyLearn, a Python project where an AI learns Flappy Bird from scratch with neuroevolution

Body:

I made FlappyLearn as an educational machine-learning project. It evolves small recurrent neural circuits to play a deterministic Flappy Bird simulator, then exports metrics, checkpoints, benchmarks, and browser-playable replays.

The project is intentionally dependency-light: Python, NumPy, pytest, and plain HTML/SVG visualizations. The docs explain neuroevolution, NEAT-inspired topology growth, genetic algorithms, mutation, crossover, fitness functions, and selection in beginner-friendly language.

What I would love feedback on:

- Clarity of the training loop and docs.
- Better visualization ideas.
- Benchmark methodology.

## LinkedIn Launch Post

I’m releasing FlappyLearn, an open-source educational project where an AI learns Flappy Bird from scratch through neuroevolution.

The project is built around a simple idea: machine learning is easier to understand when you can watch it happen. FlappyLearn evolves recurrent neural circuits, logs every generation, exports browser replays, and explains the underlying concepts in plain language.

It includes a deterministic simulator, configs, checkpoints, benchmarking, CI, tests, community docs, and guides for neuroevolution, genetic algorithms, mutation, crossover, and fitness functions.

It is designed for students, educators, developers, and anyone who wants a concrete way to explore evolutionary machine learning.

# Code Of Conduct

FlappyLearn is a learning community. We want contributors, students, educators, researchers, and curious builders to feel safe asking questions and sharing work.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps the community:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Assume good intent while still being clear about technical concerns.
- Give feedback that is specific, actionable, and respectful.
- Credit ideas, experiments, bug reports, and documentation improvements.
- Make room for beginners and people from different backgrounds.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, insults, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
- Dismissive comments about identity, background, ability, or experience level.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of issues, discussions, reviews, or community channels.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, limit participation, or ban contributors when behavior harms the community. Enforcement decisions should be proportional, documented privately by maintainers, and focused on protecting the project.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers through the project's private security contact or by emailing the maintainer team once a public contact address is configured in the repository profile. Include links, screenshots, dates, and a short explanation of the impact when possible.

Maintainers will review reports promptly, preserve confidentiality as much as practical, and avoid asking affected people to mediate directly with the person who caused harm.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, community channels, events, and any public space where someone represents FlappyLearn.
